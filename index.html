About Egyptian

Ancient Egypt is an ancient civilization in the northeast of Africa. The ancient Egyptian civilization centered on the banks of the Nile River in what is now the Arab Republic of Egypt. Ancient Egyptian civilization followed the prehistoric era and merged around 3100 BC (according to the traditional Egyptian chronology) with the political unification of Upper and Lower Egypt under Mina (also called Narmer) The history of ancient Egypt occurred as a series of stable kingdoms, separated by periods of relative instability. The many accomplishments of the ancient Egyptians include quarrying, surveying and masonry techniques that supported the construction of the pyramids and temples and huge obelisks. A system of mathematics, a practical and effective system of medicine, irrigation systems and agricultural production techniques, the first known wooden boats, Egyptian ceramics and glass technology. The success of the ancient Egyptian civilization was due in part to its ability to adapt to the farming conditions of the Nile Valley. The expected flooding of the Nile and the controlled irrigation of the fertile valley resulted in the production of surplus crops, which helped increase the density of population, social development and culture. By providing resources, the administration sponsored the exploitation of minerals in the valley and the surrounding desert areas, the early development of an independent writing system, the organization of collective building and agricultural projects, trade with the surrounding regions and an army aimed at asserting Egyptian dominance.
